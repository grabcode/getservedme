I am proud of the work done on the Utrex website. It was made for a student (General Assembly) turned client

read more/read less

On mobile, it feels like an app. For that, the aim was to get a super fast loading time of the assets and get the page repaint at 60FPS, without losing any SEO (website had to work statically too: js disabled). During the process, I've actually re-invented the "Turbolinks" (after some research I met a similar concept in ruby).

In a nutshell, the idea is to fetch the link of the page via AJAX, and extract the content you want to inject it into your "wrapper" element. Another benefit of this process is the full control over the transitions/animations between pages!

The downside atm: it requires a bit to rethink your javascript, particularly the event binding layer.

I now investigate around component, since the idea around that consists of building a self-contained piece of content/functionality. I even had to do a bit of php, language I haven't touched for 5 years.
